About The Disease

Dengue

Dengue fever is the world's fastest-growing mosquito-borne viral disease. Incidence rates have increased 30-fold over the last 50 years, geographic expansion into new countries has progressed, and over the current decade, they have expanded from urban to rural areas.

Virus

It's belongs to the family Flaviviridae, genus Flavivirus. It is a single stranded positive-sense ribonucleic acid virus.

HOW dengue IS SPREAD

Transmission of Dengue

The incidence of dengue has increased 30-fold over the last 50 years. Up to 50-100 million infections are now estimated to occur annually in over 100 endemic countries, putting almost half of the world’s population at risk.

Mosquito Bites

Dengue viruses are spread to people through the bites of infected Aedes species mosquitoes. These are the same types of mosquitoes that spread Zika and chikungunya viruses.

mother to child

A pregnant woman can pass the virus to her fetus during pregnancy or around the time of birth. there has been one documented report of dengue spread through breast milk.

infected parts

Rarely, dengue can be spread through blood transfusion, organ transplant, or through a needle stick injury.

dengue SYMPTOMS

what are they and should I call the doctor?

It's cannot be spread directly from person to person. However, a person infected and suffering from dengue fever can infect other mosquitoes. it is a mosquito-borne tropical disease caused by the dengue virus. Symptoms typically begin 3 to 14 days after infection.

According to the WHO, the most common symptoms of dengue are high fever, Severe headaches, Pain behind the eyes, Severe joint and muscle pain, Nausea or Vomiting, and Skin rash, which appears two to five days after the onset of fever.

HOW TO PREVENT YOURSELF

Prevention of Dengue

You can protect yourself, your family and help prevent spreading the virus to others if you:

Clothing

Reduce the amount of skin exposed by wearing long pants, long-sleeved shirts, and socks, tucking pant legs into shoes or socks, and wearing a hat.

Mosquito repellents

Use a repellent with at least 10 percent concentration of diethyltoluamide. Avoid using DEET on young children.

Mosquito traps and nets

Sleeping under the mosquito net can provide you and your children a double layer of protection from being bitten by mosquitoes.

Stagnant water

The Aedes mosquito breeds in clean, stagnant water. Checking for and removing stagnant water can help reduce the risk.
